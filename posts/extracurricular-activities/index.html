Extracurricular activities are just a distraction

August 4, 2021

Extracurricular activities are just a distraction from academic activities and bring students very few actual benefits. How far do you agree with this statement? Support your point of view with reasons and/or examples from your own experience.

In today’s world, designing a good school curriculum has been a heated discussion. Some educators think there are few benefits gained from extracurricular activities while these activities put extra burden on students. This essay will argue why this idea is flawed and extracurricular activities should not be overlooked.

To begin with, it is often costly to get involved in extracurricular activities. First, it is true that these activities are often time-consuming, thereby leaving students with litte time to focus on academic subjects. Without sufficient time to study, students will struggle to understand abstract concepts often seen in demanding subjects such as maths and physics. Therefore, their academic performance will be negatively affected. Second, it is believed that extracurricular activities such as sports, music, and dance clubs are more fun and sociable compared to seemingly dull but important academic subjects. It is easy for students to get hooked into these activities and they will probably forget their main duty in school. Because of these reasons, students are easily get distracted from academic activities.

Despite minor disadvantages, extracurricular activities play a crucial role in a well-rounded education. By participating in clubs and voluntary events, students have an opportunity to learn communication skills, which is beneficial when they enter the workforce. In addition, joining chess or STEM clubs can foster intellectual growth in students . Finally, doing voluntary work makes students application stand out when they apply for scholarships. Many pretigious universities consider extracurricular activies in an admission application as bonus points.

In conclusion, extracurricular activities bring a lot of benefits to the table both in short and long term. However, students need to be vigilant against overcommitting to other activities that they neglect their main role of studying.
