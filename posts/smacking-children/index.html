Smacking Children

December 11, 2020

Smacking children is the best form of discipline. To what extent do you agree or disagree?

Using physical punishment in raising children is commonplace in some demographics in society. These people believe that smacking children is the most effective means to maintain a high standard of discipline. This essay will argue why violent methods are unnecessary despite some reasonable explanations.

Smacking children has long been proven to be an effective way of getting children behaved. Some parents find physical punishment the only way to control their children’s tantrums. For example, beating rebellious children will stop their bad behaviours and will probably prevent them from misbehaving in the future. In addition, corporal punishment is likely to cause fear and force children to obey rules and orders. Because of these reasons, parents often resort to physical punishment to deal with disobedient children.

However, I believe that exploitation of physical punishment is counter-productive and detrimental in the run long. First, there is a risk that smacking seriously hurts a child due to their fragile nature. In many cases, it is reported that children are badly injured due to violent physical abuse. Second, smacking might cause long-term emotional problems. Children who suffer from domestic violence tend to behave aggressively and are more likely to develop depression, anxiety, or even antisocial habits. Finally, corporal punishment does not guarantee controlled behaviours because a child will learn nothing about self-control, discipline, and to obey the rules.

In conclusion, the drawbacks of smacking children far outweigh the seemingly potential benefits. Therefore, I firmly believe that physical punishment should be avoided at all cost and parents should seek alternative methods.

  • corporal punishment = physical punishment

  • discipline (u.n): practice of training people to obey rules

  • disobedient (adj): failing to refusing to obey

    • disobedient to somebody/something
  • smacking children
