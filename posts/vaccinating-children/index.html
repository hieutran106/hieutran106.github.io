Vaccinating Children

December 16, 2020

Vaccinating children against preventable diseases is not only unnecessary but also dangerous. To what extend do you agree or disagree?

Vaccinating children is discouraged among certain demographics in society. These people claim that vaccinations are unsafe for children’s health and as such are not needed. This essay will discuss why vaccinating against widespread diseases are extremely vital to the health of children and society, despite some potential risks.

I concede that parent’s concerns about vaccine administration are legitimate. They have always been vigilant because they are worried about injecting their children with risky and pathogenic substances inside vaccines, which could potentially make their children sick. In fact, a few vaccines contain live disease organisms such as the live polio vaccine. There have been some recorded cases in which children experience severe side effects after their administration such as allergic reaction, headache, fatigue or loss of appetite. Because of these reasons, vaccine hesitancy is likely occurring at a certain level in many countries.

Despite the minor risks associated with vaccinating children, the idea that they are unnecessary and dangerous is completely preposterous. First, many deadly diseases have been eradicated thanks to vaccination campaigns. According to WHO, polio and measure, which had killed thousand of children, have completely disappeared in recent years in the world. Second, vaccine programs have prevented vast number of deaths in many countries. The number of deaths prevented far outweigh the number of children who suffer undesirable side effects. Overall, the idea that vaccinations are unsafe and worthless can produce negative outcome for society.

In conclusion, this essay argued that people who are against vaccine programs have a flawed understanding of vaccinations and as such underestimate the benefits of vaccinating children for the whole society. I believe that vaccines are scientific breakthrough which saves the world from preventable pandemics.
