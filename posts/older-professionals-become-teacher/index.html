Older professional people to retrain as school teachers

July 9, 2021

In some countries, there have been schemes to encourage older professional people to retrain as school teachers. Do you feel that this is likely to have beneficial effects on education? Support your point of view with reasons and/or examples from your own experience.

In many countries, the scarcity of high quality teachers has put great pressure on the education system. In these countries, educational strategists come up with schemes to encourage senior members in the workforce to teach at schools. This essay will discuss why this scheme is extremely beneficial to education system and society in spite of potential disadvantanges.

To begin with, retraining older professional people as school teachers is not without problems. First, older people’s brains are likely to be hard-wired which makes it difficult for them to accquire teaching skills or change their mindset. Research has shown a young person normally spends about four years at an educational institute to become a qualified teacher, and the average time for this investment would be longer for the older. As a result, retraining would be greatly time-consuming and inefficient. Second, the generation gap would make it more challenging for the seniors to relate to young children and they might lack the pedagogical ability to convey their ideas. Furthermore, as the world is changing rapidly, senior people might not be able to catch up with new technologies to teach in today’s modern education system.

The above-mentioned disadvantages are far outweighed by the benefits that those senior skillfull citizens could bring in. First, senior professionals tend to have many years of experiences, having deep understanding of their fields of expertise. It would be great opportunities for young students to gain knowledge and experience from real experts. Second, with years of industrial experiences, these senior members would be capable of advising students both academically and professionally. For example, increasingly more universities have been inviting industry experts to deliver guest lectures in many courses. This practice exposes students to real-world experiences and varied workplace contexts.

In conclusion, this essay argued that despite generation gap and the lack of teaching skills, old professional people should be retrained as school teachers thanks to their real understanding of the workplace and the relevance of industry perspectives.
