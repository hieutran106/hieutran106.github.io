Artificial Intelligent Robots

December 8, 2020

Some people believe that eventually all jobs will be done by artificially intelligent robots. What is your opinion?

Artificial intelligent robots are gradually replacing workers in the workforce. While it is possible that all repetitive tasks will be automated by robots, I personally believe that some jobs which require human intervention will never be replaced.

On the one hand, robotic machines could undoubtedly perform mundane, labor-intensive and repetitive jobs not only quicker and cheaper but also more precise than human counterparts. It is primarily because, machines will work over the clock with great accuracy once programmed.Therefore, many companies will turn to artitificial intelligence because it is far less expensive than hiring, training, and employing humans. For example, Amazon, the biggest online retailer in the world, employs thousands of robots in their warehouses for storing and delivering packages, which costs the company much less than employing workers. For these reasons, manual and laborious tasks are likely to be replaced by robots.

On the other hand, jobs that require specific human skills could never be taken over. Critical thingking, problem solving, or emotional sympathy are skills that robots can not acquire. Only humans can look at a problem from many different perspectives and come up with an innovative solution. Also, only humans can really listen to others with emphathy and offer necessary emotional support. Thus, regardless of advancement in artifical intelligence, creative and human-related occupations such as arts, medicine, and social work are always in high demand for humans in society.

In conclusion, although lots of tasks will be overtaken by artifical intelligent machines in the future, I strongly believe that the involvement of humans in a few sophisticated occupations will remain unchanged.
